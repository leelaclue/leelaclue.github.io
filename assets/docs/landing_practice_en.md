## Six Steps to Master the Practice

The St·O·R spread only works when you follow the full cycle - from asking the honest question all the way to a daily micro-action you can actually do.

1. **Self-Clarification · The Query** - Distill your real question. Not "Should I do X?" but "What am I afraid of if I don't?" Turn outward questions inward.

2. **Synchronicity · The Draw** - Draw three cards representing the St·O·R perspectives. Randomness bypasses the ego's defenses - the 72 Leela states force a perspective your rational mind would never choose.

3. **Personal Interpretation · Inner Dialogue** - Connect each St·O·R symbol to your specific situation. Not the textbook meaning - your meaning, your pain point, your pattern.

4. **Fixation · The Journal** - Write it down. Shadow work cannot be truly accomplished without externalizing your thoughts. Insight without documentation evaporates by the next day. Your Reflection Diary holds you accountable.

5. **Self-Analysis · Reflective Practice** - Engage in what researcher [Donald Schön](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflective_practice) called "Reflective Practice." Analyze your journaled reflections for each card in the St·O·R spread to extract the core insight. Experience alone doesn't lead to learning - only purposeful reflection creates growth.

6. **Personal Discipline · The Practice** - Translate the core insight from your analysis into one hyper-specific micro-action. The practice must directly ground your discovery into daily life. Not "be calmer" - "three minutes of slow breathing before opening email."
