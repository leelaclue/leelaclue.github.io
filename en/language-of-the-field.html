The Language of the Field: A Reflection on Truth, Stillness, and New Beginnings

2026-03-30

As a facilitator of the game, I am often asked: "What exactly do you mean by the field speaking to us?" Today, I don't want to answer theoretically; instead, I'd like to take you directly into one of my own readings.

I asked LeelaClue: "What message should I share with my subscribers now?"

The answer was a powerful journey through three states of consciousness that reflect exactly what we need in today's world.

1. The Starting Point: Uranta Loka – Immersing in Stillness

My current standpoint was Uranta Loka. It is the field of contemplation and the revealing of the inner world.

In a world that's getting louder, Uranta Loka is an invitation to retreat. Before sending a message into the world, we must check if it comes from our deepest core or if it's just an echo of the noise outside.

What this means for you: Before taking the next step in your life, take a moment of silence. Only in stillness can you hear the subtle nuances of your inner truth.

2. The Blind Spot: Dharma – Acting from Inner Truth

Interestingly, the field Dharma appeared in the position of "What am I missing?". When we miss Dharma, it often means we are "functioning," but have lost contact with our true purpose.

We often confuse duties with Dharma. But Dharma is not a compulsion; it is the law of your being. When we ignore our Dharma, life feels heavy, like a struggle against the current.

What this means for you: Examine your actions. Do they align with your inner truth? Or are you trying to fulfill the expectations of others? Today, the field reminds us that success is only sustainable when it stands in harmony with our Dharma.

3. The Resource: Janma – The Birth of the New

As a solution and source of power for all of us, Janma appeared. This is the field of birth, total openness, and new beginnings.

After silence (Uranta) and alignment with truth (Dharma), the birth of something new inevitably follows. Janma is the energy of a child seeing the world for the first time – without prejudice, full of curiosity and potential.

What this means for you: We are in a phase of collective change. Janma tells us: Don't be afraid of the unknown. The time has come to let go of old concepts and welcome the "birth" of your new projects or life phases with joy.

Why this reading is so important

These three cards form a perfect bridge. They show us the process of transformation:

  • Retreat (Uranta) to gather strength.
  • Correction (Dharma) to check the direction.
  • Manifestation (Janma) to bring the new into the world.

As a leader of Leela, I see this reading as a confirmation for our community. It's time to step into this new phase of life (Janma) without masks and truthfully (Dharma).

Your exercise for today

Take a quiet look at these three fields. Which one triggers resistance in you? Where do you feel a longing?

  • Do you currently feel like seeking the stillness of Uranta Loka?
  • Do you sense that your actions no longer match your Dharma?
  • Or are you longingly waiting for the spark of Janma to finally start anew?

Feel free to write to me or send me your own reading from the app. I look forward to deciphering the messages of your personal field together with you.
